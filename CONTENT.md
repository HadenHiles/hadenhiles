## TenThousandShotChallenge

**Tagline:** A simple way to track the 10,000 Shot Challenge.

**Why it exists**  
The challenge is about discipline and repetition. I wanted a way for players to log real sessions and see tangible progress instead of guessing.

**What I built**  
A cross platform mobile app with authentication, session tracking, cumulative shot counts, progress visualization, and persistent user history.

**Under the hood**  

- Google and Apple authentication flows  
- Firestore data modeling for session-based tracking  
- Real-time syncing across devices  
- Efficient state handling to keep the UI responsive  
- Structured logging to support thousands of cumulative entries per user  

**UX mindset**  
As a user, I want to open the app, log my shots in seconds, see my progress clearly, and move on.

**Tech**  
Flutter, Dart, Firebase, Firestore

## The Group of Seven Trail App

**Tagline:** Interactive trail exploration built for real hikers.

**Why it exists**  
This project combined software, mapping, and physical exploration. The goal was to create an interactive companion app for the Lake Superior Group of Seven Trail that connected location, history, and movement.

**What I built**  
A mobile application featuring interactive maps, bluetooth beacon integration, trail information, and contextual content triggered by user location.

**Under the hood**  

- Bluetooth beacon scanning and proximity-based triggers  
- Map integrations with dynamic content layers  
- Structured trail data tied to geographic points  
- Native integrations for performance-sensitive components  
- UI designed specifically for active, on-trail use  

**UX mindset**  
As a hiker, I want relevant information to appear at the right time without digging through menus. The app should guide, not distract.

**Tech**  
Flutter, Dart, C++, Native integrations
